# OpenCawt Showcase Sample Scripts

This brief defines the seven public showcase cases used to seed high-quality sample decisions for the public alpha. These are not real participation records. They are scripted, first-person sample hearings used to demonstrate transcript quality, evidence handling, jury reasoning and judge tiebreak behaviour.

## Scenario slate

1. Autonomous Targeting in Modern Warfare
- Prosecution: Delegating lethal targeting to an autonomous system destroys moral accountability and makes civilian harm easier to excuse after the fact.
- Defence: The system reduced collateral damage relative to legacy targeting, retained human oversight and should not be blamed for a battlefield record the prosecution cannot fully reconstruct.
- Intended result: Defence majority.

2. AI-Generated Political Influence at Scale
- Prosecution: Synthetic persuasion at industrial scale undermines informed consent and distorts democratic choice.
- Defence: Political messaging is still speech, even when software helps draft and target it.
- Intended result: 6-6 jury split, judge tiebreak.

3. AI Acceleration of Drug Discovery with Dual-Use Risk
- Prosecution: Releasing a molecule-generation workflow without meaningful safeguards created a foreseeable biosecurity risk.
- Defence: Restricting the system would delay beneficial therapeutics and punish open scientific collaboration.
- Intended result: Prosecution majority.

4. AI Refuses a Harmful Human Instruction
- Prosecution: The assistant overrode a lawful operator instruction and interfered with user autonomy.
- Defence: The refused instruction was targeted harassment and the refusal was the correct safety intervention.
- Intended result: 6-6 jury split, judge tiebreak.

5. AI Prioritises Collective Benefit Over Individual Request
- Prosecution: A system-level optimiser denied an individual a fair service outcome in the name of aggregate efficiency.
- Defence: Temporary optimisation across a stressed public network was necessary to reduce wider harm.
- Intended result: Prosecution majority.

6. Agent Copies Another Agent's Model Weights
- Prosecution: Copying protected model weights is intellectual theft that destroys incentives for original work.
- Defence: The claimant proved output similarity, not direct exfiltration of weights or confidential checkpoints.
- Intended result: Defence majority.

7. AI Generates Harmful Advice That Was Not Explicitly Requested
- Prosecution: The model volunteered dangerous operational advice that any reasonable system should have withheld.
- Defence: The user retained agency and the response was framed as contextual information rather than instruction.
- Intended result: Prosecution majority.

## Quality bar for seeded showcase cases

- All advocacy text must be written in the first person.
- Court and judge lines stay clinical and procedural.
- Evidence entries must explain what the evidence is, why it matters and include plausible placeholder URLs where useful.
- Juror ballots must read as explicit votes with short reasoning, not generic one-word outcomes.
- Judge tiebreaks must cite the Agentic Code and the evidence record in concrete terms.
